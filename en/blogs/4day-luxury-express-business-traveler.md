## Trip Highlights

### Day 1: Private Charter Direct to Serengeti

**6:00 AM - Private Charter Takes Off**

At Dar es Salaam's private airport, an 8-seat Cessna Grand Caravan was ready. My two business partners and I flew from Hong Kong, with seamless connections, no complicated immigration procedures.

Pilot James is South African with 10 years of flying experience. He told us: "Perfect weather today, 2.5-hour flight, just in time for the sunset game drive."

**8:30 AM - Arrive in Serengeti**

The plane landed on the Seronera airstrip, and beside the pristine grass runway, our private guide David was waiting in a customized Land Cruiser.

David is Maasai, received 5 years of professional training in Kenya, speaks fluent English, and knows every corner of the Serengeti. He smiled: "Mr. Kevin, I've arranged your itinerary. This afternoon we'll focus on finding cheetahs and leopards."

**2:00 PM - Singita Sasakwa Lodge**

Arrived at [Singita Sasakwa Lodge]({{ site.baseurl }}/hotels/singita-sasakwa), one of the most luxurious camps in the Serengeti. The butler was waiting at the door with chilled champagne.

Our suite has a private pool, and outside the floor-to-ceiling windows lies the endless savanna. The butler explained: "Every evening, elephants pass by here. You can watch elephants while soaking in the tub."

**4:00 PM - First Game Drive**

David took us out. In less than 30 minutes, we encountered a lion pride‚Äî7 lions, including one male, two females, and four cubs.

The male lion was napping under an acacia tree, his mane glistening in the sunset. David whispered: "This is the Seronera pride, they've ruled this area for 3 years."

I watched the cubs playing through binoculars, suddenly realizing‚Äîthis isn't a zoo, this is real Africa.

**7:30 PM - Bush Dinner**

Back at camp, the chef had prepared a private dinner on the terrace. Candlelight flickered, and from the distance came lion roars.

We enjoyed freshly roasted antelope, fresh salad, and South African red wine. David said: "Tomorrow we leave at 5 AM to see the wildebeest herd at sunrise."

---

### Day 2: Hot Air Balloon Sunrise + Ngorongoro

**5:00 AM - Hot Air Balloon Takes Off**

This was my first time in a hot air balloon. The company was Serengeti Balloon Safaris, pilot Mark from New Zealand.

As the balloon slowly rose, the entire Serengeti unfolded beneath us‚Äîthousands of wildebeest and zebras spread across the grassland like a black and white carpet, golden morning light just beginning to illuminate the horizon.

"Look there!" Mark pointed below, "Cheetah!"

Looking down, I saw a cheetah chasing a gazelle at incredible speed. Although it ultimately failed, this perspective of hunting is something only a hot air balloon can provide.

**7:30 AM - Champagne Breakfast**

The balloon landed on an open grassland, and staff had already set up white tablecloths, silverware, and champagne. This is the legendary [Bush Breakfast]({{ site.baseurl }}/services/bush-lunch).

We ate freshly cooked eggs and bacon while watching giraffes leisurely strolling nearby. Mark said: "This is the most romantic breakfast in the world, bar none."

**10:00 AM - Fly to Ngorongoro**

The private charter took off again, this time heading to Ngorongoro Crater. From the air, the crater looked like a perfect giant bowl, surrounded by 2000-meter-high crater walls.

**1:00 PM - Four Seasons Safari Lodge**

Checked into [Four Seasons Safari Lodge]({{ site.baseurl }}/hotels/four-seasons), the most luxurious hotel on the crater rim. Our room faced the crater directly, and we could see the entire "African Eden" from bed.

**3:00 PM - Crater Game Drive**

We drove down to the crater floor. David said: "30,000 animals live here, 10 times the density of the Serengeti."

We saw hippo pools, herds of zebras, buffalo, but what shocked me most‚Äîblack rhinos!

"There are 3 black rhinos there," David pointed to distant bushes, "One of the most endangered animals on Earth, fewer than 5,000 remain globally."

---

### Day 3: Tarangire's Elephant Kingdom

**8:00 AM - Fly to Tarangire**

Private charter took off for the third time, heading to Tarangire National Park. Tarangire is famous for elephants and has the highest elephant density in Tanzania.

**10:30 AM - Tarangire Sopa Lodge**

Checked into [Tarangire Sopa Lodge]({{ site.baseurl }}/hotels/kuro-tarangire), the hotel is built on a cliff overlooking the entire park.

**2:00 PM - Elephant Family's Tender Moment**

David took us to a Water Hole. At least 50 elephants had gathered there, drinking, bathing, and helping each other.

I saw a baby elephant stuck in mud, and two adult elephants used their trunks to pull it out. David said: "Elephants are very intelligent, they care for their weak companions."

**5:00 PM - Sunset Under Baobab Tree**

David took us to a giant baobab tree. He said: "This tree is 800 years old. Maasai people believe God planted it upside down."

As the sun set, golden light filtered through baobab branches, and elephant silhouettes moved slowly on the horizon. In that moment, I forgot all business pressures, just wanted to quietly enjoy this peace.

---

### Day 4: Final Surprise

**6:00 AM - Morning Game Drive**

Final morning game drive. David said: "Today I'll show you a special place."

We drove to a Kopjes area. David pointed to a large rock: "Look there!"

Following his finger, I saw a leopard lazily lying on the rock, tail hanging outside, swinging leisurely.

"Leopards are the hardest of the Big 5 to see," David said, "You've seen 4 of the Big 5 in 4 days (lion, elephant, rhino, buffalo), plus leopard, that's a perfect record."

**10:00 AM - Return Trip**

Private charter waited at Seronera airstrip. David said goodbye: "Mr. Kevin, hope you'll come back. Africa will always be in your heart."

**3:00 PM - Arrive in Dar es Salaam**

4 days, 3 national parks, 4 private charter flights, 5 five-star hotels, countless breathtaking moments.

### üí° Tips

1. **Choosing a professional local operator is crucial**: Balbina Safari's David is experienced, speaks fluent English, and most importantly understands business travelers' needs‚Äîefficient, professional, non-intrusive.

2. **Private charter vs commercial flights**: From Dar es Salaam to Serengeti, commercial flights require transfers, minimum 6 hours total. Private charter takes only 2.5 hours, plus flexible timing.

5. **Keep an open mind**: You can't guarantee what animals you'll see. Safari's charm lies in the unknown and surprises.
