## Trip Highlights

### Day 1: Group Departure + Tarangire's Elephant Party

I found a group tour on Hostel World‚Äî"Budget 3-day Northern Circuit, $680 per person, max 6 people."

**10:00 AM - Enter Tarangire National Park**

Entrance fee $53/person, hurt so much! But Ali said: "Trust me, it's worth it."

Less than 5 minutes into the park, we saw our first elephant herd‚Äîat least 20, eating grass by the roadside!

I pulled out my second-hand Canon 600D (bought for $300),ÁñØÁãÇ pressing shutter. Sarah laughed: "Wang, you're so excited!"

Ali took us to public camp for lunch. Tourists from around the world at the camp, everyone greeting each other, sharing snacks.

### Day 2: Serengeti - Big 5 First Experience

**5:30 AM - Morning Game Drive**

Ali said: "Morning game drive is best time to see animals."

We got up at 5:30, departed at 6. Early morning Serengeti beautiful as a painting‚Äîgolden morning light, mist, distant mountains.

Less than 1 hour, we saw lion pride! 7 lions, napping under acacia tree.

"Look at the male lion," Ali pointed, "He's king of the savanna."

I held my breath, recording this moment with camera. First time seeing lions in the wild!

**10:00 AM - Seronera Public Camp**

We arrived at Seronera public camp to rest. Camp had showers (cold water), toilets (fairly clean), kitchen (cook yourself).

Marco and I went for water, met a group of baboons on the way. They were mischievous, trying to grab our water bottles.

"Be careful!" Ali warned, "Baboons are smart!"

**2:00 PM - Searching for Cheetahs**

Ali said: "Let's go look for cheetahs. They're harder to find."

We drove searching for 2 hours, finally saw a cheetah on a rock‚Äîblack "tear marks" clearly visible, scanning savanna.

"Cheetah is fastest animal on earth," Ali said, "It can run at 110 km/h!"

We waited 30 minutes, although it didn't hunt, seeing a cheetah was extremely lucky.

**7:00 PM - Camp Stargazing**

Nighttime Serengeti, starry sky breathtaking. Countless stars, Milky Way clearly visible.

"This is my favorite place," Ali said, "Here, you feel small."

We sat around bonfire, drinking cheap Tanzanian beer (TZS 2000/bottle, about $1), talking about life.

"You know?" Ali said, "When I was little, saw lions walking past our house every day. Now, they're getting fewer."

In that moment, I suddenly understood the meaning of wildlife conservation.

### Day 3: Ngorongoro - Final Shock

**7:00 AM - Descend to Crater**

Ngorongoro Crater entrance fee $71/person, another big expense! But Ali said: "This is Africa's most beautiful place."

We descended along zigzag road to crater floor. 600-meter vertical drop, 30 minutes of winding road.

At bottom, sight left me speechless‚Äîgiant "bowl" 20km diameter, surrounded by 2000-meter-high crater walls, inside emerald grassland, blue lakes, herds of animals.

**10:00 AM - Searching for Black Rhinos**

Ali said: "Let's try to find black rhinos. They're very rare."

We searched with binoculars for 1 hour, finally found a gray giant by distant bushes.

Although far away, seeing endangered black rhino with own eyes was extremely lucky.

**1:00 PM - Crater Picnic**

We ate lunch at designated picnic spot. Beside was hippo pool, dozens of hippos squeezed together yawning, roaring.

"Don't get too close," Ali warned, "Hippos are dangerous!"

**4:00 PM - Return Trip**

Reluctantly left crater, drove back to Arusha.

In car, I flipped through photos from these 3 days‚Äîlions, elephants, cheetahs, black rhinos, giraffes, zebras, wildebeest... each a story.

**8:00 PM - Farewell Dinner**

We had farewell dinner at a local restaurant in Arusha. Ali said: "You did great! Saw 4 of Big 5 in 3 days!"

We raised glasses‚Äîwith cheap Tanzanian beer, celebrating these 3 days of adventure, our friendship, Africa's magnificence.

## Practical Experience Sharing

### üí∞ Cost Reference
- Package Price: **$680/person** (group rate)
- Actual Cost: **$720/person** (including extra drinks and souvenirs)
- Value Assessment: **For budget travelers, this is the best value choice!**

Cost Breakdown:

- Visa: $50/person (visa on arrival)
- Tips: $30/person (to Ali)
- Extra Expenses: $16/person (beer, snacks, souvenirs)

### üéí Packing Recommendations

**Essential Gear**:
- Backpack (50-70L)
- Sleeping bag (camp provides, but your own is cleaner)
- Headlamp or flashlight (camp very dark at night)
- Sunscreen (SPF50+)
- Hat and sunglasses
- Lightweight jacket (nights cold)
- Wet wipes (camp no showers)
- Mosquito repellent (very important!)

**Optional Items**:
- Camera + lens (second-hand works)
- Power bank (camp no charging)
- Playing cards (evening entertainment)
- Harmonica or guitar (if you can play)
- Portable speaker (keep volume down, don't disturb others)

### üí° Tips

1. **Group tour is key**: Going to Safari alone is too expensive, group tour saves money. I found group info on Hostel World, Couchsurfing, Facebook Groups.

2. **Choose public camps**: Luxury camps $500+/night, public camps $20/night. Although conditions worse, you meet backpackers from around the world!

3. **Cook yourself**: Camps have kitchens, buying groceries and cooking yourself much cheaper than eating at restaurants. We 6 cooked together, under $5 per person per meal.

4. **Bring enough cash**: Many places don't accept credit cards, must bring enough USD cash.

5. **Learn to bargain**: When buying at local markets, must bargain! Usually can knock off 30-50%.

6. **Respect local culture**: Tanzanians friendly, but don't casually photograph, especially Maasai people.

7. **Keep open mind**: You'll meet people from around the world, communicate with them, you'll learn a lot.

8. **Safety first**: Camp has wildlife at night, don't go out alone, must follow guide.
