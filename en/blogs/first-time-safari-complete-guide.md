## 100 Beginner Questions Complete Answers

### üõÇ Visa Section

**Q1: Does Tanzania need visa?**
A: Yes. Chinese citizens can apply for e-visa or visa on arrival.

**Q2: How to apply for e-visa?**
A: Visit Tanzania Immigration Bureau official website, fill application form, upload passport photo, round-trip flight tickets, hotel booking, pay $50 visa fee, usually approved 3-5 working days.

**Q3: Can do visa on arrival?**
A: Yes. At Kilimanjaro International Airport (JRO) or Dar es Salaam International Airport (DAR) immigration counter, fill application form, pay $50 visa fee, usually approved within 30 minutes.

**Q4: How long is visa valid?**
A: Single entry visa valid 3 months, multiple entry visa valid 6 months.

**Q5: How long passport validity needed?**
A: Passport validity at least 6 months.

**Q6: Need yellow card (vaccination certificate)?**
A: Yes. Yellow fever vaccination certificate required, especially for travelers from or through yellow fever endemic areas.

### üíâ Vaccine Section

**Q7: What vaccines needed?**
A: Must: Yellow fever vaccine. Recommended: Typhoid, Hepatitis A, Hepatitis B, Rabies.

**Q8: Where to get yellow fever vaccine?**
A: At local International Travel Health Care Center or Entry-Exit Inspection and Quarantine Bureau.

**Q9: How much yellow fever vaccine?**
A: About ¬•200-300.

**Q10: How long yellow fever vaccine valid?**
A: Lifetime valid.

**Q11: Need malaria prevention medicine?**
A: Recommended. Consult doctor, choose suitable malaria prevention medicine (like Malarone, Doxycycline).

**Q12: How much malaria prevention medicine?**
A: About ¬•500-1000 per course.

**Q13: What medicines to bring?**
A: Cold medicine, stomach medicine, painkillers, band-aids, sunscreen, mosquito repellent, motion sickness medicine.

### üëï Packing Section

**Q14: What color clothes to wear?**
A: Khaki, olive green, brown, gray. Avoid white, black, blue, red.

**Q15: Why not white?**
A: White gets dirty easily, also reflects sunlight, makes animals alert.

**Q16: Why not black?**
A: Black absorbs heat, very hot under African sun.

**Q17: Why not blue?**
A: Blue attracts tsetse flies, they spread sleeping sickness.

**Q18: Why not red?**
A: Red angers certain animals, especially bulls.

**Q19: How many clothes to bring?**
A: Long-sleeve shirts 2-3, long pants 2-3, T-shirts 3-4, jackets 1-2, underwear 5-6 sets.

**Q20: What shoes needed?**
A: Comfortable hiking shoes or sports shoes, preferably non-slip, waterproof.

**Q21: Need hat?**
A: Yes. Wide-brimmed hat or sun hat, protect head and neck.

**Q22: Need sunglasses?**
A: Yes. UV400 protection sunglasses, protect eyes.

**Q23: Need sunscreen?**
A: Yes. SPF50+ sunscreen, reapply every 2 hours.

**Q24: Need mosquito repellent?**
A: Yes. DEET-containing mosquito repellent, reapply every 4 hours.

**Q25: What electronics to bring?**
A: Camera, phone, power bank, travel adapter (Tanzania uses UK standard plugs, 220V).

**Q26: Need binoculars?**
A: Recommended. 8x42 or 10x42 binoculars most suitable for Safari.

**Q27: Need camera?**
A: Recommended. At least 200mm telephoto lens, 300mm or longer better.

**Q28: Need tripod?**
A: Optional. If photography enthusiast, tripod improves stability.

**Q29: Need water bottle?**
A: Recommended. 1-2 liter water bottle, stay hydrated.

**Q30: Need wet wipes?**
A: Must. Camps and toilets might not have running water, wet wipes very practical.

### ü¶Å Safari Section

**Q31: Can get out of vehicle?**
A: No! Safari vehicle safest place, getting out very dangerous.

**Q32: Why can't get out of vehicle?**
A: Animals are wild, potentially aggressive. Getting out seen as threat, animals might attack.

**Q33: Can feed animals?**
A: No! Feeding animals destroys natural behavior, might cause animals to attack humans.

**Q34: Can use flash photography?**
A: No! Flash startles animals, might trigger aggressive behavior.

**Q35: Can make loud noise?**
A: No! Loud noise startles animals, affects other tourists' experience.

**Q36: Can smoke?**
A: No! Smoking not only dangerous, also pollutes environment, affects other tourists.

**Q37: Can throw trash?**
A: No! All trash must be brought back to camp or designated trash bins.

**Q38: Can get too close?**
A: No! Maintain safe distance, follow guide's advice.

**Q39: Can touch animals?**
A: No! Animals are wild, touching them very dangerous.

**Q40: Can bring pets?**
A: No! Pets startle wildlife, might be attacked by wildlife.

### üí∞ Cost Section

**Q41: How much is Safari?**
A: Budget type: $500-800/day, Mid-range: $800-1500/day, Luxury: $1500-3000+/day.

**Q42: What's included in cost?**
A: Usually includes: park fees, accommodation, meals, vehicle, guide, fuel.

**Q43: What's not included in cost?**
A: Usually not includes: international flights, visa, tips, personal expenses, alcoholic beverages.

**Q44: How much for tips?**
A: Guide: $15-25/day, Driver: $10-20/day, Camp staff: $5-10/day.

**Q45: How to give tips?**
A: Cash best, USD or Tanzanian shillings both OK.

**Q46: Need to give tips?**
A: Yes. Tips are main income source for guides and staff.

**Q47: How much are park fees?**
A: Serengeti: $71/day, Ngorongoro: $71/day, Tarangire: $53/day.

**Q48: Can buy multi-day tickets?**
A: Yes. Can buy 24-hour tickets, or multi-day tickets.

**Q49: Any discounts on tickets?**
A: Yes. East African Community (EAC) citizens enjoy discounted rates, international tourists full price.

**Q50: Need to book in advance?**
A: Yes. Peak season (July-October) needs booking 2-3 months ahead.

**Q78: Will vehicles break down?**

### üõ°Ô∏è Safety Section

**Q81: Is Tanzania safe?**
A: Generally safe. But stay vigilant, avoid going out at night, don't display valuables.

**Q82: Is Safari safe?**
A: Safe. As long as follow guide's advice, don't get out of vehicle, don't feed animals.

**Q83: Are there dangerous animals?**
A: Yes. Lions, elephants, rhinos, buffaloes, leopards are Big 5, all dangerous.

**Q84: Will encounter animal attacks?**
A: Rarely. As long as maintain distance, follow guide's advice, usually won't.

**Q85: Is there malaria?**
A: Yes. Recommend taking malaria prevention medicine, use mosquito repellent.

**Q86: Are there other diseases?**
A: Yes. Recommend getting necessary vaccines, maintain hygiene.

**Q87: Need to buy travel insurance?**
A: Yes. Travel insurance can cover medical expenses, emergency evacuation, lost luggage.

**Q88: How much travel insurance?**
A: About ¬•500-1000/person, depends on coverage and duration.

**Q89: Need to bring first aid kit?**
A: Recommended. Bring some basic medicines and medical supplies.

**Q90: Are there medical facilities?**
A: Arusha and Dar es Salaam have hospitals, but not inside parks.

**Q91: Need to give tips?**

A: Guides, drivers, camp staff, baggage handlers.

**Q97: Can give gifts to locals?**
A: Yes. But recommend giving practical items, like stationery, clothes.

**Q98: Can give money to locals?**
A: Not recommended. Giving money creates dependency, recommend supporting local community projects.

**Q99: Need to speak Swahili?**
A: No. English is official language, but learning some Swahili helpful.

**Q100: What's most important point?**
A: Respect nature, respect animals, respect local culture.
