## Luxury Experience

### Day 1-2: Singita Sasakwa Lodge - Serengeti Luxury

**Day 1: Arrive at Singita Sasakwa Lodge**

Singita is Africa's most luxurious safari brand, with 3 camps in the Serengeti‚ÄîSasakwa, Faru Faru, Sabora.

I chose Singita Sasakwa Lodge, Singita's most luxurious camp in the Serengeti, located on a cliff in western Serengeti, overlooking the entire savanna.

Our butler was James, a Kenyan with 10 years of luxury service experience.

"Welcome to Singita Sasakwa Lodge," James smiled, "This is Africa's most luxurious camp."

**Suite Luxury**

Our suite had a private infinity pool facing the Serengeti savanna. Through floor-to-ceiling windows, we could see herds of wildebeest and zebras slowly moving on the horizon.

"This is our honeymoon suite," James said, "$2,500 per night."

The suite included:
- Private pool (infinity pool, overlooking savanna)
- Floor-to-ceiling windows (facing savanna)
- Private bathroom (bathtub, shower, double sink)
- Private balcony (with lounge chairs and dining table)
- Private bar (with various wines)
- Private safe
- Air conditioning (central AC)
- Wi-Fi (high-speed)
- Telephone (international direct dial)

**Private Butler Service**

James was our private butler, serving us 24 hours a day.

"I can help you with anything," James said, "Book game drives, arrange dinner, prepare bathwater, iron clothes."

James's services included:
- 24-hour standby
- Booking game drives
- Arranging dinner
- Preparing bathwater
- Ironing clothes
- Shoe shining service
- Wake-up service
- Luggage service

**Michelin-Level Dining**

Singita's dining was Michelin-level. Dinner included:
- Appetizer: Foie gras with truffles
- Main course: Roasted antelope with red wine sauce
- Dessert: Chocolate mousse with vanilla ice cream
- Wine: South African red wine, champagne

"This is our chef," James pointed to a French chef, "He worked at a Michelin three-star restaurant."

**Infinity Pool Romance**

In the evening, we swam in the private pool, watching the sunset, golden light spilling over the savanna.

"This is my most romantic moment," James said, "Watching the sunset in the infinity pool."

### Day 5-7: Other Luxury Camps - Northern Circuit Luxury

**Day 5: Lamai Serengeti**

Lamai Serengeti is a luxury camp in northern Serengeti, located in the Kogatende area, near the Mara River.

Our suite had a private balcony facing the Mara River. We could see wildebeest and zebras crossing the river.

"This is our river crossing suite," the butler said, "$1,800 per night."

**Day 6: Sayari Camp**

Sayari Camp is a mobile luxury camp in northern Serengeti, moving with the Great Migration.

Our tent had a private balcony facing the savanna. We could see herds of wildebeest and zebras slowly moving on the horizon.

"This is our migration suite," the butler said, "$1,500 per night."

**Day 7: One Nature Nyaruswiga**

One Nature Nyaruswiga is a luxury camp in central Serengeti, located in the Central Seronera area.

Our suite had a private infinity pool facing the savanna. We could see lions, elephants, giraffes.

"This is our central suite," the butler said, "$2,200 per night."
