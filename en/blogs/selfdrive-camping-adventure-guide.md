## Self-Drive Camping Experience

### Day 1: Arrive in Arusha - Preparation for Departure

**10:00 AM - Arrive in Arusha**

We arrived in Arusha from Kilimanjaro International Airport (JRO). The rental company was already waiting at the airport.

"Welcome to Tanzania," the rental company employee said, "This is your Land Cruiser."

Our Land Cruiser was modified, with a roof tent, refrigerator, gas stove, water bucket, toolbox.

"This is your car," the rental company employee said, "It's ready."

**2:00 PM - Buy Supplies**

In the afternoon, we went to the supermarket to buy supplies‚Äîwater, food, gasoline, camping gear.

"You need to prepare enough supplies," the rental company employee said, "The Northern Circuit doesn't have many supermarkets."

We bought:
- Water: 20 liters
- Food: Bread, eggs, vegetables, fruit, meat
- Gasoline: 100 liters
- Camping gear: Tent, sleeping bag, pad, lights

**6:00 PM - Check Vehicle**

In the evening, we checked the vehicle‚Äîtires, oil, brakes, lights.

"The vehicle is ready," the rental company employee said, "You can depart tomorrow."

---

### Day 2: Tarangire National Park - First Game Drive

**7:00 AM - Depart**

On the second day at 7 AM, we departed for Tarangire National Park. From Arusha to Tarangire is about 2.5 hours drive.

"This is your first game drive," the rental company employee said, "Drive carefully."

**9:30 AM - Arrive at Tarangire**

At 9:30 AM, we arrived at Tarangire National Park. We paid the park entrance fee, then entered the park.

"Welcome to Tarangire," the park staff said, "Watch out for elephants."

**10:00 AM - First Game Drive**

We began our first game drive. Tarangire is most famous for elephants and baobab trees.

"Look, elephants!" I shouted excitedly.

We saw an elephant family of about 30, slowly crossing the road.

"This is the largest elephant herd," I said, "It's shocking."

**12:00 PM - Lunch**

At noon, we had lunch at the park's picnic site. Lunch was prepared by ourselves‚Äîsandwiches, fruit, water.

"This is the simplest lunch," I said, "But it's delicious."

**2:00 PM - Continue Game Drive**

In the afternoon, we continued game driving. We saw baobab trees, lions, giraffes, zebras.

"This is the most beautiful baobab tree," I said, "It might be over 1000 years old."

**6:00 PM - Check into Campsite**

In the evening, we checked into a public campsite. The public campsite has toilets, showers, kitchen, but no electricity.

"This is the simplest campsite," I said, "But it's comfortable."

We spent the night in the roof tent. The roof tent was comfortable, with a pad, sleeping bag, lights.

"This is the most comfortable camping," I said, "Better than I imagined."

### Day 3: Serengeti National Park - Endless Plains

On the third day at 6 AM, we departed for Serengeti National Park. From Tarangire to Serengeti is about 2 hours drive.

"This is the most beautiful lion pride," I said, "It's shocking."

### Day 5: Ngorongoro Crater - African Eden

**6:00 AM - Depart**

On the fifth day at 6 AM, we departed for Ngorongoro Crater. From Serengeti to the crater is about 2 hours drive.

"This is the steepest road," I said, "But it's worth it."

**8:00 AM - Arrive at Crater**

At 8 AM, we arrived at Ngorongoro Crater. We paid the park entrance fee, then began descending.

"Welcome to the crater," the park staff said, "Watch out for black rhinos."

**9:00 AM - African Eden**

We began descending to the crater floor. The road was very steep, zigzagging mountain road, 600 meters vertical drop, took about 30 minutes.

After descending to the bottom, the scene before me was shocking beyond words:

A huge "bowl" 20 kilometers in diameter, surrounded by 2000-meter high crater walls. Inside the bowl were emerald green savanna, blue lakes, herds of animals... This is why people call it "African Eden."

"This is the most beautiful crater," I said, "It's shocking."

**12:00 PM - Lunch**

At noon, we had lunch at the crater floor picnic site. Lunch was prepared by ourselves‚Äîsandwiches, fruit, water.

"This is the simplest lunch," I said, "But it's delicious."

**2:00 PM - Black Rhinos**

In the afternoon, we saw black rhinos. Black rhinos are endangered species, about 30 black rhinos live in the crater.

"Look, black rhinos!" I shouted excitedly.

Although far away, through binoculars we could clearly see their signature two horns and huge size.

"This is the most precious animal," I said, "I'll never forget."

**6:00 PM - Return to Arusha**

In the evening, we returned to Arusha. From crater to Arusha is about 3 hours drive.

"This is the longest drive," I said, "But it's worth it."

### üéí Packing Recommendations

**Essential Equipment**:
- International driver's license (required)
- GPS (required)
- Map (backup)
- Camping gear: Tent, sleeping bag, pad, lights
- Kitchen gear: Gas stove, pot, bowl, chopsticks
- Food: Bread, eggs, vegetables, fruit, meat
- Water: 20 liters

### üí° Tips

1. **International driver's license**: Must obtain an international driver's license, otherwise cannot drive in Tanzania.

2. **GPS**: Must bring GPS, otherwise easy to get lost.

3. **Public campsites**: Must book public campsites in advance, especially during peak season.

4. **Cook yourself**: Cooking yourself can save a lot of money, and it's delicious.

5. **Encountering animals**: When encountering animals, maintain distance, don't get out of the car, don't feed them.

6. **Stay safe**: Stay safe, don't drive too fast, don't drive at night.

7. **Record everything**: Take photos, videos, write diaries, these records are precious self-drive camping memories.
