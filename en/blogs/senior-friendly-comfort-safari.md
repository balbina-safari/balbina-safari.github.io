## Senior-Friendly Safari

### Day 1: Arrive in Arusha - Adapting to the Environment

**10:00 AM - Arrive in Arusha**

We arrived in Arusha from Kilimanjaro International Airport (JRO). The travel agency was already waiting at the airport.

"Welcome to Tanzania," the travel agency employee said, "This is your driver and guide."

Our driver and guide was Moses, a Maasai with 15 years of Safari experience.

"Welcome," Moses smiled, "I will take good care of your parents."

**2:00 PM - Check into Hotel**

In the afternoon, we checked into the hotel. The hotel was accessible, with elevators, accessible bathrooms, and accessible paths.

"This is an accessible hotel," Moses said, "Your parents will be comfortable."

**4:00 PM - Rest**

In the afternoon, we let our parents rest. Long flights are tiring, need time to adapt.

"Let your parents rest," Moses said, "We'll start tomorrow."

**6:00 PM - Dinner**

In the evening, we had dinner at the hotel. Dinner was light, suitable for elderly appetites.

"This is a light dinner," Moses said, "Your parents will like it."

### Day 4: Ngorongoro Crater - The Highlight

**6:00 AM - Depart**

On the fourth day at 6 AM, we departed for Ngorongoro Crater. From Arusha to the crater is about 3 hours drive.

"This is the highlight," Moses said, "Ngorongoro is African Eden."

**9:00 AM - Arrive at Crater**

At 9 AM, we arrived at Ngorongoro Crater. We paid the park entrance fee, then began descending.

"Welcome to the crater," the park staff said, "Watch out for black rhinos."

**10:00 AM - African Eden**

We began descending to the crater floor. The road was very steep, zigzagging mountain road, 600 meters vertical drop, took about 30 minutes.

After descending to the bottom, the scene before my parents was shocking beyond words:

A huge "bowl" 20 kilometers in diameter, surrounded by 2000-meter high crater walls. Inside the bowl were emerald green savanna, blue lakes, herds of animals... This is why people call it "African Eden."

"This is the most beautiful crater," my father said, "It's shocking."

**12:00 PM - Lunch**

At noon, we had lunch at the crater floor picnic site. Lunch was prepared by the travel agency‚Äîsandwiches, fruit, water.

"This is the simplest lunch," Moses said, "But it's delicious."

**2:00 PM - Black Rhinos**

In the afternoon, we saw black rhinos. Black rhinos are endangered species, about 30 black rhinos live in the crater.

"Look, black rhinos!" my father shouted excitedly.

Although far away, through binoculars we could clearly see their signature two horns and huge size.

"This is the most precious animal," my father said, "I'll never forget."

**4:00 PM - Return to Hotel**

At 4 PM, we returned to the hotel. Moses drove slowly, giving parents time to rest.

"Today was the highlight," Moses said, "Your parents adapted well."

**6:00 PM - Dinner**

In the evening, we had dinner at the hotel. Dinner was celebratory, we celebrated my parents' Safari trip.

"This is a celebratory dinner," Moses said, "Congratulations on your Safari trip."
