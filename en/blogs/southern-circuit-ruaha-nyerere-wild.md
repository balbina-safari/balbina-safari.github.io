## Southern Circuit Exploration

### Day 1: Arrive in Ruaha - Elephant Kingdom

**10:00 AM - Arrive in Ruaha**

We arrived in Ruaha National Park by a 2-hour flight from Dar es Salaam. Ruaha is Tanzania's largest national park, over 20,000 square kilometers.

"Welcome to Ruaha," the pilot said, "This is true wilderness."

Ruaha is remote, no tourists, only wildlife. This is true wilderness.

**2:00 PM - Check into Camp**

In the afternoon, we checked into camp. The camp was small, only 6 tents, but comfortable.

"This is the most private camp," the camp manager said, "You won't see other tourists."

**4:00 PM - First Game Drive**

In the afternoon, we began our first game drive. Ruaha is most famous for elephants.

"Look, elephants!" I shouted excitedly.

We saw an elephant family of about 50, slowly crossing the road.

"This is the largest elephant herd," I said, "It's shocking."

Ruaha has over 10,000 elephants, Tanzania's largest elephant kingdom.

**6:00 PM - Return to Camp**

In the evening, we returned to camp. The camp was quiet, only bird calls and animal sounds.

"This is the quietest camp," I said, "It's shocking."

### Day 2: Ruaha - Elephant Kingdom

**6:00 AM - Morning Game Drive**

On the second day at 6 AM, we departed for morning game drive. Early morning Ruaha is another world.

We saw more elephants. Ruaha's elephants are special, their tusks are larger than elephants elsewhere.

### Day 4: Nyerere - Boat Safari

**6:00 AM - Depart**

On the fourth day at 6 AM, we departed for Nyerere National Park. From Ruaha to Nyerere is about 2 hours drive.

"This is the longest drive," the guide said, "But it's worth it."

**8:00 AM - Arrive at Nyerere**

At 8 AM, we arrived at Nyerere National Park. Nyerere is Tanzania's largest national park, over 50,000 square kilometers.

"Welcome to Nyerere," the park staff said, "This is true wilderness."

Nyerere is remote, no tourists, only wildlife. This is true wilderness.

**9:00 AM - Boat Safari**

We began our boat safari. Nyerere is most famous for boat safaris.

"This is the most unique safari," the guide said, "You'll see animals from the boat."

We saw hippos, crocodiles, elephants, buffaloes, and various birds.

"Look, hippos!" I shouted excitedly.

We saw dozens of hippos crowded in a mud pool, constantly yawning, roaring, pushing each other.

"This is the most interesting hippos," I said, "They're so cute."

**12:00 PM - Lunch**
