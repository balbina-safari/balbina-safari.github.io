## Price Comparison

### Tanzania

**Safari Price**: **$200-300/day/person**

- Budget: $150-200/day/person (public camps, public vehicles)
- Mid-range: $200-300/day/person (private camps, private vehicles)
- Luxury: $500-1000/day/person (luxury camps, private vehicles)

**Park Entrance Fees**:
- Serengeti: $60/person/day (non-residents)
- Ngorongoro: $70/person/day (non-residents)
- Tarangire: $45/person/day (non-residents)

**Accommodation Prices**:
- Budget: $50-100/night (public camps)
- Mid-range: $200-400/night (private camps)
- Luxury: $800-2000/night (luxury camps)

### Kenya

**Safari Price**: **$150-250/day/person**

- Budget: $100-150/day/person (public camps, public vehicles)
- Mid-range: $150-250/day/person (private camps, private vehicles)
- Luxury: $400-800/day/person (luxury camps, private vehicles)

**Park Entrance Fees**:
- Maasai Mara: $80/person/day (non-residents)
- Amboseli: $60/person/day (non-residents)
- Nakuru: $60/person/day (non-residents)

**Accommodation Prices**:
- Budget: $30-80/night (public camps)
- Mid-range: $150-300/night (private camps)
- Luxury: $600-1500/night (luxury camps)

### Price Comparison Summary

**Kenya is cheaper**, about 20-30% cheaper than Tanzania.

But Tanzania's park entrance fees are higher because the parks are larger, more primitive, and more private.

## Tourist Density Comparison

### Tanzania

**Tourist Density**: **Low**

- Serengeti: 15,000 square kilometers, only 200,000 tourists per year
- Ngorongoro: 260 square kilometers, only 100,000 tourists per year
- Tarangire: 2,850 square kilometers, only 50,000 tourists per year

**Advantages**:
- Fewer tourists, better privacy
- Higher animal density, see more animals
- More primitive savanna, not commercialized

**Disadvantages**:
- Higher prices
- Less convenient transportation
- Fewer accommodation options

### Kenya

**Tourist Density**: **High**

- Maasai Mara: 1,510 square kilometers, 300,000 tourists per year
- Amboseli: 392 square kilometers, 150,000 tourists per year
- Nakuru: 188 square kilometers, 100,000 tourists per year

**Advantages**:
- Lower prices
- More convenient transportation
- More accommodation options

**Disadvantages**:
- More tourists, worse privacy
- Lower animal density, see fewer animals
- More commercialized savanna

### Tourist Density Comparison Summary

**Tanzania is more private**, fewer tourists, higher animal density.

But Kenya is more convenient, with more convenient transportation and more accommodation options.

## Visa Convenience Comparison

### Tanzania

**Visa**: **$50, visa on arrival available**

- Visa on arrival: $50, can be obtained at the airport
- E-visa: $50, can be applied online
- Visa-free: Not applicable

**Advantages**:
- Visa on arrival is convenient, no need to apply in advance
- Reasonable price

**Disadvantages**:
- Need passport (valid at least 6 months)
- Need yellow fever vaccination certificate (if coming from yellow fever endemic areas)

### Kenya

**Visa**: **$50, e-visa available**

- E-visa: $50, must be applied online
- Visa on arrival: Not applicable
- Visa-free: Not applicable

**Advantages**:
- E-visa is convenient, can be applied online
- Reasonable price

**Disadvantages**:
- Must apply in advance, cannot get visa on arrival
- Need passport (valid at least 6 months)
- Need yellow fever vaccination certificate (if coming from yellow fever endemic areas)

### Visa Convenience Comparison Summary

**Tanzania is more convenient**, visa on arrival available, no need to apply in advance.

But Kenya's e-visa is also convenient, can be applied online.

## Great Migration Viewing Comparison

### Tanzania

**Great Migration**: **Visible Year-Round**

- January-March: Southern calving season, see thousands of baby wildebeest
- April-May: Southern migration, see spectacular Great Migration
- June-July: Western migration, see spectacular Great Migration
- August-October: Northern migration, possibly see Mara River crossing (not guaranteed!)
- November-December: Southern migration, see spectacular Great Migration

**Advantages**:
- Visible year-round, no specific time needed
- Longer viewing time, more opportunities

**Disadvantages**:
- Mara River crossing not guaranteed

### Kenya

**Great Migration**: **Visible July-October**

- July-August: Northern migration, see spectacular Great Migration
- September-October: Mara River crossing, see spectacular Great Migration

**Advantages**:
- More likely to see Mara River crossing
- More concentrated viewing time

**Disadvantages**:
- Only visible July-October, not visible other times
- Shorter viewing time, fewer opportunities

### Great Migration Viewing Comparison Summary

**Tanzania is visible year-round**, no specific time needed, longer viewing time.

But Kenya's Mara River crossing is more likely to be seen, with more concentrated viewing time.
