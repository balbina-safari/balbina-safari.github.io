## Ultimate Tanzania Grand Tour

### Day 1: Arrive in Arusha - Start of the Grand Tour

### Day 2-4: Serengeti National Park - Endless Plains

**Day 2: Arrive in Serengeti**

On the second day at 7 AM, we departed for Serengeti National Park. From Arusha to Serengeti is about 6 hours drive.

"This is the longest drive," David said, "But it's worth it."

In the afternoon, we arrived in Serengeti. We paid the park entrance fee, then entered the park.

"Welcome to Serengeti," the park staff said, "Watch out for lions."

**Day 3: Serengeti Full Day Game Drive**

On the third day, we began our full day game drive in Serengeti. Wake up at 5 AM, depart at 6 AM, return at 6 PM.

"This is the longest game drive," David said, "But it's worth it."

We saw lions, elephants, giraffes, zebras, wildebeest, cheetahs, leopards, and the Great Migration.

"This is the richest wildlife," I said, "It's shocking."

**Day 4: Serengeti Full Day Game Drive**

On the fourth day, we continued our full day game drive in Serengeti. Wake up at 5 AM, depart at 6 AM, return at 6 PM.

We saw more of the Great Migration‚Äîthousands of wildebeest and zebras slowly moving on the horizon.

"This is the most shocking scene," I said, "I'll never forget."

---

### Day 5: Ngorongoro Crater - African Eden

**Day 5: Ngorongoro Crater**

On the fifth day at 6 AM, we departed for Ngorongoro Crater. From Serengeti to the crater is about 2 hours drive.

"This is the highlight," David said, "Ngorongoro is African Eden."

In the morning, we began descending to the crater floor. The road was very steep, zigzagging mountain road, 600 meters vertical drop, took about 30 minutes.

After descending to the bottom, the scene before me was shocking beyond words:

A huge "bowl" 20 kilometers in diameter, surrounded by 2000-meter high crater walls. Inside the bowl were emerald green savanna, blue lakes, herds of animals... This is why people call it "African Eden."

"This is the most beautiful crater," I said, "It's shocking."

We saw black rhinos, hippos, lions, elephants, buffaloes, and various birds.

"This is the richest wildlife," I said, "It's shocking."

---

### Day 6: Tarangire National Park - Elephant Kingdom

**Day 6: Tarangire National Park**

On the sixth day at 7 AM, we departed for Tarangire National Park. From Ngorongoro to Tarangire is about 3 hours drive.

"This is the elephant kingdom," David said, "Tarangire has the most elephants."

In the morning, we arrived at Tarangire. We paid the park entrance fee, then entered the park.

"Welcome to Tarangire," the park staff said, "Watch out for elephants."

We saw an elephant family of about 50, slowly crossing the road.

"This is the largest elephant herd," I said, "It's shocking."

We also saw baobab trees, lions, giraffes, zebras.

"This is the most beautiful baobab tree," I said, "It might be over 1000 years old."

**Day 7: Arrive in Ruaha**

**Day 8: Ruaha Full Day Game Drive**

On the eighth day, we began our full day game drive in Ruaha. Wake up at 5 AM, depart at 6 AM, return at 6 PM.

We saw elephants, lions, leopards, African wild dogs, and various birds.

"This is the most endangered animal," I said, "It's precious."

African wild dogs are endangered species, Ruaha is Tanzania's best African wild dog viewing site.

**Day 9: Ruaha Full Day Game Drive**

On the ninth day, we continued our full day game drive in Ruaha. Wake up at 5 AM, depart at 6 AM, return at 6 PM.

We saw more elephants‚ÄîRuaha has over 10,000 elephants, Tanzania's largest elephant kingdom.

"This is the largest elephant kingdom," I said, "It's shocking."
