## Wildlife Photography Experience

### Day 1: Arrive in Serengeti - Start of Photography Journey

**10:00 AM - Arrive in Serengeti**

We arrived in Serengeti from Kilimanjaro International Airport (JRO). The photography instructor was already waiting at the airport.

"Welcome to Serengeti," the photography instructor said, "This is your photography instructor."

Our photography instructor was Mark, an award-winning photographer with 20 years of wildlife photography experience.

"Welcome," Mark smiled, "I will teach you how to shoot National Geographic-level photos."

**2:00 PM - Check into Camp**

In the afternoon, we checked into camp. The camp was small, only 6 tents, but comfortable.

"This is your photography camp," Mark said, "We start the photography journey tomorrow."

**4:00 PM - Theory Class**

In the afternoon, Mark gave us our first theory class‚Äîcamera settings.

"Camera settings are the key to success," Mark said, "You must master camera settings."

We learned:
- ISO: 100-400 (sunny), 400-800 (cloudy), 800-1600 (dusk)
- Shutter speed: 1/1000 sec (moving animals), 1/500 sec (stationary animals), 1/250 sec (scenery)
- Aperture: f/4-f/5.6 (animals), f/8-f/11 (scenery)
- Focus: Continuous autofocus (AF-C), single point focus
- White balance: Auto (AWB), sunny (Daylight), cloudy (Cloudy)

**6:00 PM - Dinner**

In the evening, we had dinner at the camp. Dinner was traditional Tanzanian food‚Äîroasted meat, rice, vegetables.

"This is traditional Tanzanian food," Mark said, "You'll like it."

### Day 7: Serengeti - 10 Common Mistakes

**Day 7: Mistakes Theory**

On the seventh day at 8 AM, Mark gave us our fifth theory class‚Äî10 common mistakes.

"Avoiding common mistakes is the key to success," Mark said, "You must know what mistakes are."

We learned 10 common mistakes:
1. **ISO too high**: ISO too high produces noise, affecting photo quality
2. **Shutter too slow**: Shutter too slow causes photo blur, especially when photographing moving animals
3. **Aperture too large**: Aperture too large causes depth of field too shallow, subject not clear
4. **Focus not accurate**: Focus not accurate causes subject blur, photo not clear
5. **Poor composition**: Poor composition causes photo not attractive
6. **Poor light**: Poor light causes photo not layered
7. **Wrong lens selection**: Wrong lens selection causes photo not ideal
8. **Over post-processing**: Over post-processing causes photo not natural
9. **No patience**: No patience causes missing best shooting opportunity
10. **Disrespect animals**: Disrespecting animals causes animals to leave, unable to photograph

**Day 7: Mistakes Practice**

On the seventh day at 2 PM, we departed to avoid common mistakes. We photographed:
- ISO: Use correct ISO, avoid noise
- Shutter: Use correct shutter speed, avoid photo blur
- Aperture: Use correct aperture, ensure appropriate depth of field
- Focus: Use correct focus, ensure subject clear
- Composition: Use correct composition, ensure photo attractive

"This is the most beautiful photos," Mark said, "You avoided all common mistakes."
