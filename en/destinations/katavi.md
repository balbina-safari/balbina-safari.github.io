Katavi National Park is Tanzania's **third-largest national park** (4,471 square kilometers), but also likely the **least visited**.

Here there are no luxury hotels, no tourist crowds, not even paved roads. Coming to Katavi means returning to the real, original, undisturbed Africa. If you want to experience "Safari with only me and the animals," Katavi is the only answer.

Beyond hippos, Katavi is famous for its **breathtaking buffalo herds**.

On the Chada floodplains, you can sometimes see more than **1,000 buffalo** moving in a dark mass, kicking up clouds of dust. Buffalo herds of this scale are rarely seen elsewhere in East Africa.
