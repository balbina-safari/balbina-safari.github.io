### ğŸµ Face to Face with Chimpanzees

Mahale Mountains National Park is one of only two places in Tanzania where you can track wild chimpanzees (the other is Gombe Stream).

About **800-1,000 chimpanzees** live here, including one group called the **"M Group"** that has been studied and habituated to humans for over 40 years. This means when you follow guides into the forest, you have a **95%+ success rate** of finding and observing them at close range.

### ğŸŒ³ Rainforest Tracking Expedition

Chimpanzee tracking usually begins at dawn:
1.  **Departure**: Guides start searching based on where chimpanzees built their nests the previous day.
2.  **Trekking**: Hike through dense tropical rainforest, climbing over vines and stepping on slippery leaves.
3.  **Encounter**: When you hear hooting and branch-breaking from the treetops, look upâ€”chimpanzees are just meters above!

You can observe them:
*   Using sticks to dig for termites.
*   Mothers with babies leaping between trees.
*   Adult males challenging for dominance, beating their chests.
*   The entire family grooming each other on the ground.

By regulation, observation time is limited to **1 hour**, but that hour provides a lifetime of memories.

Mahale's highest peak reaches 2,462 meters, covered in dense tropical rainforest.

Beyond chimpanzees, the park is home to:
*   **Red Colobus Monkeys**: With flame-bright fur.
*   **Blue Monkeys**.
*   **300+ bird species**, including many endemics.
