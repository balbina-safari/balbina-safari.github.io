### ğŸš— Dar es Salaam's Backyard

Mikumi National Park is Tanzania's **fourth-largest national park** and the **most easily accessible wildlife reserve from Dar es Salaam**.

Driving along the Dar-Iringa highway for just **4 hours** brings you to this 3,230-square-kilometer wilderness. This makes Mikumi the top choice for weekend getaways for capital residents and the perfect starting point for international visitors on the Southern Circuit.

Mikumi's heart is the vast **Mkata Floodplain**â€”this open grassland resembles the Serengeti in landscape: golden grass stretching to the horizon, dotted with sparse umbrella acacia trees, with the Rubeho and Uluguru mountain ranges on the horizon.

*   **Dry Season (June-October)**: Large numbers of wildebeest, zebras, impalas, and buffalo gather on the plains.
*   **Rainy Season (November-May)**: Though roads may be muddy, this is the golden season for birdwatching, with migratory birds bringing over 400 species.

### ğŸ¦’ High-Frequency Wildlife Sightings

While Mikumi's total wildlife count doesn't match Serengeti or Ruaha, due to the park's relatively small size and concentrated animal distribution, **sighting rates are very high**:

*   **Elephants**: Moving in herds across the plains or drinking at waterholes.
*   **Giraffes**: Elegantly feeding among acacia trees.
*   **Lions**: Often sunbathing in the central area of Mkata Plains.
*   **Baboons and Vervet Monkeys**: Playing in trees along roadsides.

### ğŸŒ³ Diverse Ecosystems

Beyond open grasslands, Mikumi also includes:
*   **Miombo Woodland**: Tanzania's southern characteristic savanna woodland ecosystem.
*   **Riverine Forest**: Growing along the Mkata River banks, where hippos reside.
*   **Hippo Pools**: One of the park's signature attractions, offering close-up observation of hippos and crocodiles.
