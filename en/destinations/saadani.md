### ğŸ–ï¸ Safari on the Beach

Saadani National Park is the **only** place in East Africa that perfectly combines a **wildlife reserve with Indian Ocean beaches**.

Imagine this scene: Morning, walking along golden sand beach, warm water at your feet, you turn your head and see a herd of elephants leisurely emerging from palm forest to drink by the sea. This magical combination exists only in Saadani.

Although Saadani's wildlife density doesn't match Serengeti or Ruaha, it offers a **completely different perspective**:

*   **Beach Elephants**: Elephant herds often cross the beach, moving between seawater and freshwater estuaries.
*   **Lion Tracks on Sand**: While sighting rates aren't high, you might discover lion footprints on morning beachesâ€”they patrol their territory at night, passing along the beach.
*   **Buffaloes, Antelopes**: Grazing in grasslands near the coastline.

### ğŸš¤ Wami River Safari

Another park highlight is the **Wami River** boat safari.

This river winds through mangrove swamps before flowing into the sea. Cruising upstream in a small boat:
*   **Hippos** yawn in the wide channel.
*   **Nile Crocodiles** sunbathe on riverbanks.
*   **Hundreds of Waterbirds**: Pelicans, fish eagles, kingfishers, mangrove kingfishers, African fish eagles... a birdwatcher's paradise.

### ğŸ¢ Sea Turtle Nesting Sites

Saadani's beaches are important nesting grounds for **Green Turtles**.

*   **Best Time**: May-August, when female turtles come ashore at night to dig nests and lay eggs.
*   If lucky, with a guide (using red-light flashlights), you can observe this ancient natural ritual.
